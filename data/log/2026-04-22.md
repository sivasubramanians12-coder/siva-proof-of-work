title: Warehouse controls tightened so material flow stays visible and clean
tags: [ops, product, ux]
---
Today's shipped work landed in Hillary's Ops OS, focused on a plain operational problem: once a job moves into inbound and GRN, the team needs instant clarity on whether each purchase order has actually arrived. A new PO Delivery Status tracker was added to the Warehouse Inbound GRN tab, searchable by JID, with each PO's vendor, PO number, PO date, expected delivery, actual delivery, and rolled-up counts for delivered, pending, and awaiting PO number. That turns a scattered status check into one screen the warehouse team can act from.

The second fix addressed a more dangerous edge case. Deleting a purchase order after GRN completion could leave orphaned tailor-assignment records downstream, which meant the system could show false work in Tailor Handover even when no valid material flow remained. The fix now blocks PO deletion once any linked GRN is marked complete, and tells the team to reverse the GRN in Warehouse first. A one-time cleanup migration also removes only pristine orphaned tailor assignments, so in-progress downstream work is left untouched.

The technical decisions were practical and tightly tied to the business process. The delivery tracker reuses JID lookup and calculates expected dates from vendor TTM plus delivery overrides, so visibility sits inside the existing operating rhythm instead of becoming another report. The deletion guard is enforced in SQL, not just in the form UI, which is the right place for a rule that protects cascade integrity. Net effect: better inbound visibility, fewer ghost exceptions, and less chance of procurement cleanup corrupting downstream workflow.
