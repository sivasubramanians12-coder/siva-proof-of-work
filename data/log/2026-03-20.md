Today was a site and record cleanup: two major projects that had been live in repos but invisible on the portfolio were properly surfaced and documented. SiteCapture (the SalesCapture repo) — a field check-measure PWA for Hillarys India — was added to the site with full context: 69 commits across 4 days, 9 sprints, iPad offline-first, customer signature capture, and PDF quote parsing. Vayu, the breathwork PWA, was updated to reflect its complete arc — 10 sprints to v2.0.0, the origami UI redesign, and its production state as an offline-first health tool.

The Hillary's Ops OS case entry was also updated to reflect the advance payment and rework pipeline work, bringing the sprint count to 13+. Four backfill log entries were written covering March 12, 16, 17, and 18 — capturing the Vayu UI redesign, SiteCapture initial build, and Hillary's sprint progress that had been built but not logged. Both repos were pushed and the Vercel site rebuilt.

The business problem this solves is deceptively simple: the work exists but wasn't legible. For the portfolio to serve as proof of work — for advisory conversations, hiring, or future clients — it has to reflect what actually shipped, not just what was formally announced. That gap between "built" and "documented" is what today closed.

Today also marks the end of the 7-post LinkedIn intro series. The daily wrap cron itself was identified as flawed — it was writing from memory files only, blind to GitHub commits, which led to fabricated logs on past days. A fix has been scoped: wire the GitHub events API into the cron so it reads actual commits. That work is queued.
