Today’s work was about removing friction from systems that get used repeatedly, not adding flashy surface area. On the planning side, TravelCanvas was corrected to support the real Day 16 departure date for the Japan trip, then updated so the Narita Express leg, the Narita to Bangalore flight, and the departure-day note behave like normal draggable and deletable custom entries. That closes the annoying edge case where the last day of a trip becomes a workaround instead of a usable part of the plan.

The itinerary was then reshaped live around real family constraints. Gayatri’s office visit, Siddharth’s aquarium slot, and the Imperial Palace cycling block were moved to the right days and re-verified so May 25 and May 27 now reflect the actual intended flow. The value here is operational. When a shared trip plan can absorb changes cleanly, it stops being a source of coordination debt.

Under the hood, BarleyBot’s memory stack also moved from the builtin retrieval path to QMD-backed recall and was then expanded across all seven agents plus direct and group chats. That improves continuity across longer threads and reduces the amount of repeated context needed to get useful output. The only GitHub pushes visible across the tracked repos today were the daily log updates in `siva-proof-of-work` and `siva-builds`, which turned these operational improvements into a durable record.
