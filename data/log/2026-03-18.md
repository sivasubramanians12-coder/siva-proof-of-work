SiteCapture reached its final sprint today. Sprint v9 (admin polish) shipped, and the official Hillarys brand guidelines were applied across the full app.

The branding work was more than a colour swap. Hillarys India has a specific visual identity — typography, colour values, spacing — that now runs consistently across both Ops OS and SiteCapture. The two apps look and feel like they belong to the same system, which matters when the same CX team uses both daily.

**69 commits across 4 days.** From PRD to fully deployed field PWA — scaffold, auth, 4 role flows, offline support, photo capture, PDF parsing, customer signature, 9 sprints, admin polish, and brand application.

**What SiteCapture means for Hillary's:** The check measure visit now has a system of record. Every window has photos. Every measurement is verified. Customer sign-off is digital and attached to the PDF. When the installer arrives for installation, the full site context — photos, confirmed measurements, product specs, customer preferences — is in the system. Rework from miscommunication has a structural fix.

The Hillarys India stack is now complete: InSite (sales/design) → SiteCapture (check measure + customer alignment) → Ops OS (order lifecycle and operations).
