title: Tightening the Money Handshake — Payment accuracy, GRN fail handling, and procurement visibility in Hillary's Ops OS

Four commits to Hillary's Ops OS today, all in the payment and procurement layer. The theme: making the system more honest about money.

The biggest functional change is a 0.5% paid buffer added to CX and Installation collection checks. In practice, payment amounts rarely land exactly — rounding differences and bank charges mean a "fully paid" order often shows ₹12 short. Without a buffer, the system held these in collection queues indefinitely. The fix unblocks real orders that were stuck on a rounding artefact. Related: the Installation view now uses the order's own invoice and advance figures as the source of truth, rather than deriving them from linked PO data — which meant discrepancies caused by rework or delayed sub-orders were silently inflating balance amounts shown to the installation team.

On the procurement side, two smaller but operationally meaningful changes. First, a Vendor Order Number field was added to PO forms — procurement teams were tracking this in WhatsApp and notebooks, creating a lookup gap when deliveries arrived without paperwork. Now it's captured in the system at PO creation. Second, delivered POs without a PO number in AX3 (the ERP) are now surfaced in the Pending AX3 bucket — previously invisible, creating a reconciliation gap between the ops system and the ERP.

Separately today: the Ops OS User Guide was completed — a 29,844-byte training document covering all four teams (CX, Procurement, Warehouse, Installation), the full order lifecycle, and the JID naming convention. The -GF and -D suffix definitions were also formally corrected: -GF is a GRN Fail sub-order (not "Ground Floor"), -D is a Delayed sub-order (not a location tag). Both are system-generated, not CX-entered — an important distinction for how teams understand and handle exception orders.
